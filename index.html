A Tribute to Grace Hopper

A Pioneer in Computer Science

A recent picture of Grace Hopper, depicted showcasing her military service

Below are some major accomplishments of Hopper during her career:

  • 1906: Born on December 9 in New York City.
  • 1928: Graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Vassar College with a bachelor's degree in mathematics and physics.
  • 1930: Earned a master's degree in mathematics from Yale University.
  • 1931: Began teaching mathematics at Vassar College.
  • 1934: Received a Ph.D. in mathematics from Yale University; her dissertation was titled "New Types of Irreducibility Criteria."
  • 1943: Joined the United States Navy Reserve during World War II.
  • 1944: Assigned to the Bureau of Ships Computation Project at Harvard University as a lieutenant, junior grade, working on the Mark I computer.
  • 1949: Joined the Eckert–Mauchly Computer Corporation as a senior mathematician, contributing to the development of the UNIVAC I.
  • 1952: Developed the first compiler, known as the A-0 System.
  • 1954: Named the company's first director of automatic programming; her department released some of the earliest compiler-based programming languages, including MATH-MATIC and FLOW-MATIC.
  • 1959: Participated in the Conference on Data Systems Languages (CODASYL) as a technical consultant, contributing to the development of COBOL, a machine-independent programming language based on English words.
  • 1967–1977: Served as the director of the Navy Programming Languages Group in the Navy's Office of Information Systems Planning.
  • 1973: Promoted to the rank of captain in the U.S. Navy.
  • 1983: Promoted to commodore (later renamed rear admiral, lower half).
  • 1986: Retired from the Navy at the age of 79 as the oldest active-duty commissioned officer at that time.
  • 1991: Received the National Medal of Technology.
  • 1992: Passed away on January 1 and was interred with full military honors at Arlington National Cemetery .
  • 2016: Posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama.
